Dal Chawal

Dal Chawal is a classic Indian dish featuring a comforting combination of lentil soup (dal) and steamed rice (chawal). Rich in flavor and nutrition, dal is often spiced with turmeric, cumin, and coriander, while chawal provides a neutral, fluffy base. Together, they create a wholesome, satisfying meal.

  • 1 cup split red lentils (masoor dal), yellow lentils (toor dal), or any preferred type
  • 3-4 cups water, for cooking the lentils
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 large tomato, chopped or pureed
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, minced or grated
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit or chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1-2 tablespoons oil or ghee, for tempering
  • A handful of fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional, for a tangy flavor)
  • 1 cup basmati or any preferred variety of rice
  • 2 cups water, or as needed for the type of rice
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee (optional, for flavor)
  • 1 bay leaf (optional, for aroma)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds (optional, for added flavor)
  1. Prepare the Dal:
    • Rinse and wash the lentils thoroughly.
    • In a pot, cook the lentils with 3-4 cups of water until they are soft and fully cooked.
  2. Prepare the Tempering:
    • Heat 1-2 tablespoons of oil or ghee in a separate pan.
    • Add 1/2 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them sizzle.
    • Add 2-3 minced garlic cloves, 1-inch minced ginger, and 1-2 chopped green chilies. Saut√© until aromatic.
    • Add 1 medium finely chopped onion and cook until golden brown.
    • Add 1 large chopped or pureed tomato and cook until soft.
    • Stir in 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon coriander powder, 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder, and salt to taste. Cook for a few minutes.
    • Add 1/2 teaspoon garam masala (optional) and mix well.
  3. Combine Dal and Tempering:
    • Mix the prepared tempering into the cooked lentils.
    • Simmer for a few minutes to blend the flavors.
    • Adjust seasoning and consistency as needed.
  4. Prepare the Rice:
    • Rinse 1 cup of rice until the water runs clear.
    • In a pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil.
    • Add the rinsed rice, salt, and optional oil or ghee.
    • Reduce heat, cover, and cook until rice is tender and water is absorbed. Fluff with a fork.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve the hot dal over or alongside the steamed rice.
    • Garnish with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lemon juice, if desired.
